# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Blog readers — people arriving via AT Protocol discovery, search, or direct links to read long-form posts. They're here for the writing, not the chrome. Context: reading on desktop, tablet, and mobile, often from social/feed referrals. The job to be done is reading and, optionally, discovering related content.

Secondary audience: Zola users who fork this template as a starting point for their own sites. They need clear structure, sensible defaults, and room to customize.

## Product Purpose

A Zola starter template for AT Protocol long-form publishing that gets out of the way. It implements the standard.site lexicons (publication verification, document linking, discovery hints) so indexers and AT Protocol clients can surface content — but the reading experience comes first. Success is a site where the design is invisible and the writing holds attention.

## Brand Personality

Quiet, literary, minimal. The design whispers; the writing speaks. Three words: **restrained, typographic, warm**.

Emotional goal: calm focus. The reader should feel the site recede and the text fill the room. No friction, no decoration, no "look at this design."

## Anti-references

- **Cluttered / ad-heavy news sites** — sidebars, widgets, popups, content fighting for attention.
- **AI-default blog aesthetic** — cream/sand body background, gradient hero sections, identical card grids, tiny uppercase tracked eyebrow text above every section, numbered section markers (01 / 02 / 03) as scaffolding. These patterns are saturated and read as "AI made this."
- **Over-designed personal blogs** — elaborate animations, glassmorphism, decorative borders, gradient text. If the design draws attention to itself, it's failed.
- **Dense typography** — walls of text with no breathing room, narrow line-height, missing hierarchy.

## Design Principles

1. **The writing is the interface.** Every design decision starts from the question: does this help the reader stay with the words? If not, remove it.

2. **Restraint over decoration.** One typeface well-set beats three typefaces with effects. One accent color used deliberately beats a palette searching for purpose. Absence is a design tool.

3. **Warmth through craft, not chroma.** The site should feel human — but through typographic care, rhythm, and spacing, not through beige backgrounds or decorative warmth. Let the writing carry the personality.

4. **Standard over clever.** AT Protocol integration should feel native to the web, not bolted on. Semantic HTML, valid feeds, accessible markup. The template teaches by being correct.

5. **Shareable by default.** Someone forking this template should get a site that looks intentional out of the box — not a blank canvas that requires design work to feel finished.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target: WCAG 2.2 AA compliance
- Semantic HTML throughout — correct heading hierarchy, landmark regions, ARIA where needed
- Body text contrast ≥ 4.5:1 against background; large text ≥ 3:1
- Keyboard-navigable, screen-reader friendly
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` — no autoplay animations
- Responsive: readable on phones without horizontal scroll, comfortable line-length on wide screens
